Coding the Reverse Complement Algorithm

Sometimes when you're studying DNA, you need to find what is called the reverse complement. DNA occurs as a double strand where each A is paired with a T and each G is paired with a C. The reverse complement of a DNA sequence is formed by reversing the letters with their pair, interchanging A and T and interchaning C and G. After interchaning all of the nucleotides of the DNA strand, you take your new strand and reverse the whole thing so the last nucleotide now becomes the first nucleotide in the strand.
