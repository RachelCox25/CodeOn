What is DNA?

DNA, or deoxyribonucleic acid, is the hereditary material in humans and almost all other organisms. Most DNA is in the cell nucleus, and between two people, 99% of their DNA is the same. The information on the DNA is stored in four base pairs A, T, G, and C. The structure of DNA is like a twisted ladder—called a “double helix.” It’s formed by the bases linking together, A with T, G with C.

DNA is the start of knowing what someone will look like. That’s what we call the central dogma of biology, how DNA goes to RNA and then to proteins. (You’ll learn about RNA and proteins next).
