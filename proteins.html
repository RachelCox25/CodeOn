What are proteins?

Following the process of transcription, the RNA is decoded to produce an amino acid chain. Each amino acid is an organic compound that builds to create a protein. Just like flour is an ingredient to make cookies, an amino acid is an ingredient in a recipe to make a protein. Proteins perform a variety of different functions in different organisms like responding to stimuli or transporting other molecules. We couldn't live without them!

To make an amino acid, a RNA strand undergoes a process called translation. In translation, the RNA strand is split into groups of three nucleotides called codons. The process can begin once a start codon (AUG) is reached on the RNA strand and continues codon to codon until it reaches a stop codon. Each codon is then translated and equal to an amino acid.
